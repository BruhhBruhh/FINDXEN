XEN

General

Mission: XEN aims to be a community-building crypto asset uniting enthusiasts with the lowest entry barrier via unique tokenomics (p. 2). Designed by the Fair Crypto Foundation, it adheres to blockchain tenets: decentralization, transparency, counterparty risk resistance, P2P value exchange, and self-custody.

Problem Solved: XEN tackles the bipolar crypto market—overbought known coins and undiscovered, pre-mined unknowns—through a fair launch with no pre-mining or insider advantages (p. 2).

Tokenomics and Philosophy

Uniqueness: XEN is an ERC-20 token with minimal code, no pre-minting, admin keys, or origin wallets. It starts at zero supply, minted freely via Ethereum-compatible wallets (e.g., MetaMask) (p. 3).

Supply Dynamics: No max cap; inflationary initially, turning disinflationary as adoption grows logarithmically. Value ties to minting difficulty, akin to Bitcoin (p. 4-5).

Proof of Participation (PoP): Rewards use \( R_u = \log_2(cR_G - cR_u) * T * AMP(t s_G) * (1 + EAA(cR_u)) \), where \( cR_G \) is global rank, \( cR_u \) is user rank, \( T \) is term, \( AMP \) drops from 3,000 daily, and \( EAA \) starts at 10%, decreasing 0.1% per 100,000 ranks (p. 5-6).

Minting XEN

Process: Claim a CryptoRank (\( cR_u \)) via `claimRank(term)` (1 to freeMintTermLimit days). Post-term, claim via `claimMintReward()` within 7 days or face penalties (0% day 0, 99% day 7+) (p. 7-8, 13).

Formula: \( R_u = \log_2(cR_G - cR_u) * T * AMP(t s_G) * (1 + EAA(cR_u)) \). FreeMintTerm starts at 100 days, increasing as \( 100 + \log_2(cR_G) * 15 \) after 5,000 participants (p. 7).

Staking XEN

Process: Stake via `stake(amount, term)` (1-1,000 days). APY starts at 20%, drops 1% every 90 days to 2%. Rewards: \( R_u = \frac{S_u * APY * \tau}{100 * 365} \). Early withdrawal returns stake only; post-maturity includes APY (p. 8-9, 14).

Technology

Blockchain: XEN runs on Ethereum mainnet as an ERC-20 token, leveraging its $570B peak market cap security (p. 10).

First Principles: Inspired by crypto-anarchism and Bitcoin’s P2P ethos, XEN resists centralization trends (e.g., CBDCs) with a trustless design (p. 11).

The Cause

Vision: XEN revisits blockchain’s roots—consensus-driven, people-powered—against centralized corruption in crypto (p. 11).
